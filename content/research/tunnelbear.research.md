## Content Gaps in Top SERP Pages

1. **Audit-cadence angle underexplored.** Top results mention audits but rarely emphasize that TunnelBear is the ONLY major VPN with 9 consecutive annual audits from the same firm (Cure53, 2017–2025). This is genuinely rare discipline and should be the trust-counterweight to McAfee/5-Eyes concerns.
2. **The "McAfee + 5-Eyes" problem framed constructively is rare.** Most reviewers either ignore it (fluff) or treat it as automatic disqualification. The honest middle ground — "concerning, but the audit discipline counterbalances it" — is largely absent.
3. **January 2026 free-plan change** — TunnelBear stripped server-selection and split-tunneling from free tier. Fresh news; most 2026 reviews haven't updated.
4. **Canadian-VPN comparison** — Windscribe is also Canadian. Head-to-head Canadian VPN comparison is a gap.

## Review angle (playbook)

- **Verdict** opens with the McAfee + 5-Eyes disclosure paired IMMEDIATELY with the 9-year audit cadence as the empirical counterweight.
- **Speed** honest (mid-tier, weak on Windows/OpenVPN).
- **Security** leads with audit cadence (unique in industry), addresses encryption/protocols, covers GhostBear + VigilantBear, addresses jurisdiction with nuance.
- **Streaming** honest (TunnelBear's own help page says it doesn't reliably unblock Netflix).
- **Pricing** simple tiered table + free-tier context (2GB is tiny vs Windscribe 10GB / Proton unlimited).
- **Comparison** vs NordVPN (mainstream), vs Windscribe (fellow-Canadian, better free), vs ProtonVPN (privacy-first alternative).
- **Who should pick TunnelBear** — beginners who value audit transparency and a friendly interface over raw performance.
- **FAQ** — answer the 8–12 most-searched PAAs verbatim.
