## Content Gaps in Top SERP Pages

1. **2025 Dutch seizure as redemption-arc closer** — Most review pages still end the "trust" discussion at the 2021 Ukraine incident. The 2025 Dutch incident (RAM-only servers, nothing recoverable) is the empirical proof that the 2021 remediation worked. This pairing is underused and is a genuine EEAT angle.
2. **Build-A-Plan math** — Nearly every review mentions Build-A-Plan as "flexible pricing" but few actually walk through the numbers ($1/location, minimum $3, +$1 for unlimited data). Showing a real scenario ("expat who needs US + UK + Canada + unlimited = $4/month") beats the vague framing.
3. **Greek court case** — The April 2025 Greek court dismissal (Bruce Schneier covered it, TechRadar wrote it up) is the strongest court-proven no-logs evidence in the VPN industry alongside Mullvad's 2023 raid. Most reviews mention it in one line; it deserves a proper paragraph.
4. **Free-tier data math** — 10 GB = ~3 hours of HD Netflix, or ~30 hours of browsing. Few reviews translate this into something users understand.
5. **R.O.B.E.R.T. category granularity** — Most reviews say "it blocks ads and trackers." Few list the actual categories (ads, trackers, social, porn, gambling, cryptominers, fake news/clickbait, malware) or explain that it works at DNS level across every app.
6. **Canadian jurisdiction nuance** — The 5-Eyes concern is valid but reviews tend to either ignore it or FUD it. The correct framing: jurisdiction matters less when there are no logs to subpoena (proven by the Greek court case).
7. **Mac vs Windows speed gap** — Security.org documented that Mac speeds are significantly worse than Windows. Very few reviews flag this platform-specific caveat.
8. **Windflix servers** — Rarely explained that Windscribe ships dedicated streaming servers separate from their standard fleet.

## Key Competitor Positioning (for the `vs` sections)

- **vs ProtonVPN (Swiss, privacy-first):** Proton wins on jurisdiction (Switzerland, no 5-Eyes) and unlimited-data free tier. Windscribe wins on server variety in free tier (10 countries vs 3), unlimited devices (vs 1), and streaming on paid.
- **vs Mullvad (no-affiliate, privacy-max):** Mullvad wins on audits, privacy-theater-free approach, cash payments, flat pricing. Windscribe wins on streaming, server count, and usability for beginners.
- **vs NordVPN (mainstream):** NordVPN wins on speed, server count, independent audit cadence, RAM-only infrastructure maturity. Windscribe wins on free tier (NordVPN has none) and pricing flexibility.

## Tone Prescription

Journalist-who-actually-tested. Acknowledge the 2021 incident in a dedicated section, not buried in a pros/cons bullet. Pair the 2025 Dutch seizure with it to show the remediation worked. Address Canadian jurisdiction without FUD — point to the court case as the empirical answer. Be honest that Mac performance lags Windows. Recommend Windscribe to the right user (budget, multi-device, streaming on Pro, free-tier dabblers) and recommend Mullvad/NordVPN to the wrong user (max-privacy, speed-critical, enterprise).
