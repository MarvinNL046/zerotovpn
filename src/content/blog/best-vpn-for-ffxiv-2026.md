## How Routing Optimization Actually Works

Here is why a VPN can reduce ping in FFXIV even though it adds an extra hop:

**Without VPN (bad ISP routing):**
Your PC → ISP Node 1 → ISP Node 2 → Congested peering point → ISP Node 3 → Transit provider → Square Enix server

**With VPN (optimized routing):**
Your PC → VPN server (nearby) → VPN backbone (uncongested) → Square Enix server

The VPN provider's backbone network bypasses the congested peering points that cause your latency spikes. This is why VPNs help some players dramatically and do nothing for others — it depends entirely on whether your ISP has bad routing to Square Enix's infrastructure.

## When a VPN Will Not Help

Be realistic about what a VPN can and cannot do:

- **Physical distance still matters.** Playing on Japanese servers from Europe will always have 200+ ms ping. A VPN might bring it from 280 ms to 220 ms, but it cannot break the laws of physics.
- **Local network issues are not fixable.** If your latency comes from Wi-Fi interference, a bad Ethernet cable, or router problems, a VPN changes nothing.
- **Good ISP routing needs no VPN.** If your ping is already clean and stable, adding a VPN will only increase it by a few milliseconds.
- **Free VPNs make things worse.** Overloaded servers and limited locations mean free VPNs almost always add latency for gaming.

## VPN vs. Dedicated Game Tunneling Services

FFXIV players sometimes use dedicated tunneling services like Mudfish or ExitLag instead of traditional VPNs. These services focus exclusively on game traffic routing and tend to add less overhead. However, they do not encrypt your full connection or offer the privacy benefits of a VPN.

If your only goal is reducing FFXIV ping and you do not care about privacy, a tunneling service is a viable alternative. If you want both gaming optimization and general-purpose VPN protection, NordVPN or ExpressVPN cover both needs.

## FAQ

### Does Square Enix allow VPNs in FFXIV?
Yes. Square Enix does not ban players for using VPNs. Many players on official forums openly discuss VPN use for reducing latency, and there has never been a reported ban for VPN usage alone.

### Which VPN protocol is best for FFXIV?
WireGuard-based protocols (NordLynx, Lightway, or native WireGuard) offer the lowest latency. OpenVPN works but adds more overhead. Avoid IKEv2 for gaming — it is stable but slower than WireGuard.

### Can a VPN fix the 90002 disconnect error?
The 90002 error is usually caused by unstable routing or packet loss between you and Square Enix servers. A VPN can fix this if the instability is on your ISP's side by providing a more reliable route. Many players on the FFXIV forums report that VPNs resolved persistent 90002 issues.

### Should I use a VPN if I play on my region's data center?
Test it. Even same-region players can have bad ISP routing. Try a few roulettes with the VPN on and off. If your ping drops or stabilizes, keep it on. If it increases, your ISP routing is already fine.

### Can I use a VPN on PlayStation 5 for FFXIV?
There is no native VPN app for PS5. You need to either install the VPN on your router or share your PC's VPN connection via Ethernet. Router installation is the most reliable method for console players.

### How much ping improvement should I expect?
It varies. Players with bad ISP routing (common on certain US and EU providers) can see 20-80 ms improvement. Players with already clean routing might see 0-5 ms added latency. The only way to know is to test.
