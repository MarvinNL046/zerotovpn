## How MLB.TV Blackouts Work in 2026

MLB.TV blackouts exist because local TV broadcast rights are sold by territory. When your local RSN (Regional Sports Network) or broadcaster has exclusive rights to air a game, MLB.TV is contractually required to black it out in your area.

**What gets blacked out:**
- Your local team's games, live, if you are in their defined home market
- Nationally broadcast games on ESPN, Fox, TBS, or Apple TV+ (blacked out on MLB.TV for everyone)
- Games become available on MLB.TV 90 minutes after they end

**2026 changes:** MLB has expanded in-market streaming for 21+ teams through direct team subscriptions, reducing the scope of blackouts for some markets. However, many fans still face blackouts, particularly those in overlapping market territories (like Iowa, which is blacked out for six different teams).

To check your blackout situation, enter your ZIP code on [MLB's blackout checker](https://www.mlb.com/live-stream-games/subscribe).

## Smart DNS vs. Full VPN for MLB.TV

Most top VPNs offer both a full VPN mode and a Smart DNS (or "MediaStreamer") mode. Here is how they differ for MLB.TV:

**Full VPN:**
- Encrypts all traffic
- Changes your IP address completely
- Works on any device with a VPN app
- May slightly reduce streaming speeds
- Better for privacy

**Smart DNS:**
- Only reroutes location-checking traffic
- Does not encrypt your connection
- Works on devices without VPN app support (smart TVs, consoles, Apple TV)
- No speed reduction
- Less private

For MLB.TV on a smart TV or Apple TV, Smart DNS is usually the better choice because it is easier to set up and does not affect streaming quality. For watching on a phone, tablet, or computer, the full VPN is more versatile.

## FAQ

### Is it legal to use a VPN with MLB.TV?
Using a VPN is legal in the US. However, bypassing MLB.TV's blackout restrictions may violate their terms of service. MLB does not actively pursue individual users for this, but they can technically terminate your MLB.TV subscription if they detect VPN use. In practice, this is rare.

### Will MLB.TV detect my VPN?
MLB.TV does attempt to detect and block VPN IP addresses. This is why choosing a VPN with a large server network is important — providers like NordVPN and ExpressVPN cycle their IPs regularly to stay ahead of blocks. If one server is detected, switching to another usually works.

### Can I use a VPN with the MLB.TV app on my phone?
Yes. Install the VPN app on your phone, connect to a server outside your blackout zone, then open the MLB.TV app. Both the iOS and Android MLB.TV apps work with VPN connections.

### Does a VPN work for MLB.TV on Roku or Fire TV?
For Roku, you need to set up Smart DNS on your router or use the VPN at the router level, since Roku does not support VPN apps. Fire TV Stick supports VPN apps directly — NordVPN, Surfshark, and ExpressVPN all have Fire TV apps.

### What about the new MLB in-market streaming options?
As of 2026, 21 MLB teams offer direct in-market streaming subscriptions that let local fans watch without blackouts. If your team offers this option, it may eliminate your need for a VPN. Check [MLB.com](https://www.mlb.com/live-stream-games/subscribe) for your team's availability.

### Can I watch nationally broadcast games on MLB.TV with a VPN?
No. Games on ESPN, Fox, TBS, or Apple TV+ are blacked out on MLB.TV for all subscribers regardless of location. A VPN does not help because the blackout is universal, not geographic. You need to watch these games on the broadcast network's own app or channel.
